About Me

I am a Computer Science PhD student at Old Dominion University in Norfolk, Virginia, advised by Professor Dr. Frank Y. Liu. My research focuses on Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning, with an emphasis on AI foundation models.My academic journey began in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Thessaly (UTH), where I earned an Integrated Master’s degree (a combined bachelor’s and master’s program). In addition, I completed a Certificate of Expertise in Data Science, considered equivalent to a minor in the American system. I conducted my thesis under the supervision of Dr. Elias Houstis.

As an undergraduate, I served as a Teaching Assistant for courses such as Problem-Solving Environments and Data Science, Machine Learning for Data Science and Analytics, and Applied Statistics. These experiences provided me with an early introduction to academic life and deepened my interest in pursuing research.

Outside of academics, I enjoy cooking, often experimenting with new recipes, and working out at the gym, both of which help me maintain balance.
