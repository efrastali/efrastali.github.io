About Me

I was born into a family of educators and musicians, where my interests in academics and music developed early. In the Panhellenic exams, I ranked in the top 3% nationwide in mathematics, which led me to study Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Thessaly, graduating in the minimum five years (average seven). Alongside my degree, I completed a Certificate of Expertise in Data Science with advanced coursework in machine learning and statistics. During my studies, I gained experience in Assembly, MySQL, R, C, C++, Java, and Python, building a solid foundation in database management, computer architecture, and parallel computing. My focus later shifted toward machine learning, leading to Teaching Assistant roles in Machine Learning, Problem Solving Environments for Machine Learning, and Introduction to Statistics. Currently, I am a Graduate Research and Teaching Assistant at Old Dominion University, working with Dr. Dushan Wadduwage (Harvard FAS). My research now centers on computational imaging, optics, and differentiable microscopy, integrating my background in machine learning with advanced imaging applications.
